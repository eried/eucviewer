EVIDENCE+WEATHER Estimated full range

Range over time (median per group)
Range vs ambient temperature WEATHER (one dot per trip)

Each trip's range is extrapolated from distance vs battery used (distance / battery% × 100). Dots are the distance-weighted median per group, the band spans the 25th to 75th percentile. Trips using less battery than the threshold above are excluded. Logger-tail corruption (frozen voltage / battery after wheel shutdown) is auto-trimmed before computing battery delta.

EVIDENCE Charging events

Charges per group (detected from battery jumps between rides)
State of charge per top-up (start → end %)

A charge is detected whenever consecutive trips show the battery jumping back up (next trip's start > previous trip's end). Cycling from a high state of charge (e.g. 80 → 100) is gentler on Li-ion packs than deep drains; cycling from low (15 → 100) accelerates aging.

EVIDENCE Top speed

Top speed over time (max + median per group, GPS-verified)
Speed distribution across rides (trip count by top-speed band)

GPS-verified speeds: samples where the wheel speed disagrees with ground motion (i.e. free spins) are excluded so a pickup test or fall can't poison the lifetime max.

FINDING Acceleration

Best 0 → 40 km/h time per group (seconds, lower is faster)

For every ride we scan for runs where the speed climbed from a near-stop up to 40 km/h (or 60 in the second chart). Anomalous samples (free spins or physics-impossible accelerations) are skipped so a wheel lifted off the ground can't masquerade as a launch run. Shorter times mean the wheel had more headroom — useful for spotting pack sag with age.

ANOMALY Free spins & suspected falls

Detection thresholds i

A free spin is a sample where the wheel reported a speed but GPS shows the wheel didn't actually move. Most are pickup tests, but a few are falls: when the wheel was already moving fast on the ground and then suddenly its speed disagreed with GPS while ground speed crashed. Tune the thresholds above to match your wheel.

EVIDENCE Efficiency

Energy use over time (median per group)
Power-draw distribution (trip count by avg-W band)

Energy per distance (Wh/km, computed from logged power or voltage×current) and distance per battery percent (km/%). The histogram shows how hard you typically push the wheel: most riders sit in a narrow average-power band with a long tail toward steep climbs / sprints.

EVIDENCE Motor: speed vs current

Avg speed vs avg current (one dot per trip, old → new)
Current efficiency over time (amps per unit of speed)

Top: each dot is one trip, average moving speed against average (positive) current, coloured from oldest (blue) to newest (red). If recent dots sit above older ones at the same speed, the drivetrain is drawing more amps than it used to. Bottom: median amps per km/h per group, a speed-independent view of the same drift.

EVIDENCE+WEATHER Thermal

Temperature rise vs power draw WEATHER (one dot per trip, old → new)
Temperature rise over time (median per group)

Top: peak board temperature rise above ambient versus average power, one dot per trip (oldest blue, newest red). Bottom: median temperature rise per group. A drifting-up trend at similar power means the wheel is heating more than it used to.

EVIDENCE Battery health: voltage sag

Effective internal resistance over time (median per group, mΩ)
Voltage droop over the trip (% sag from peak, lower is healthier)

Two independent battery-aging proxies. Effective IR is the slope of voltage against current using only short-timestep deltas (so SoC drift across the trip cancels out). Voltage droop is the gap between the pack's peak resting voltage and its lowest loaded voltage during the same ride — naturally widens as the pack ages.
