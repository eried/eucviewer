Each trip's range is extrapolated from distance vs battery used (distance / battery% × 100). Dots are the distance-weighted median per group, the band spans the 25th to 75th percentile. Efficiency

Energy use over time (median per group)

Energy per distance (Wh/km, computed from logged power or voltage×current) and distance per battery percent (km/%). Rising Wh/km or falling km/% means the wheel needs more energy for the same ride.

Motor: speed vs current

Avg speed vs avg current (one dot per trip, old → new)
Current efficiency over time (amps per unit of speed)

Top: each dot is one trip, average moving speed against average (positive) current, coloured from oldest (blue) to newest (red). If recent dots sit above older ones at the same speed, the drivetrain is drawing more amps than it used to. Bottom: median amps per km/h per group, a speed-independent view of the same drift.

Thermal

Temperature rise vs power draw (one dot per trip, old → new)
Temperature rise over time (median per group)

Top: peak board temperature rise above ambient versus average power, one dot per trip (oldest blue, newest red). Bottom: median temperature rise per group. A drifting-up trend at similar power means the wheel is heating more than it used to.

Battery health: voltage sag

Effective internal resistance over time (median per group, mΩ)

Effective internal resistance estimated per trip from how much the pack voltage sags per amp of load (robust fit of voltage against current). A rising trend indicates an aging battery pack.
